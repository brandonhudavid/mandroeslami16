About Me
  Aloha! I am a fifth year PhD candidate in the field of Geotechnical Earthquake Engineering at University of California, Los Angeles. My PhD research topic is investigation of monotonic and cyclic behavior of fine grained soils; silt clay mixtures with varying pore fluid conditions. I have also conducted studies on numerical verification and constitutive modelling of sandy soils, trying to experimentally obtain the yield surface and plastic potential surfaces of sands using triaxial experiments. I strive to understand soils and structural behavior under seismic conditions since my childhood where I had passion for earthquakes and volcanoes, which has led me to choose this journey in life.
